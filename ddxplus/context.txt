spontaneous pneumothorax:  Spontaneous pneumothorax is the sudden collapse of lung tissue due to air leakage into the chest cavity, causing sharp chest pain, coughing, and shortness of breath.
cluster headache:  Cluster headaches: severe unilateral head pain, often around eye; tearing, nasal congestion, agitation; can be chronic or episodic.
boerhaave:  Boerhaave Disease: a rare, life-threatening esophageal rupture, causing severe chest pain, vomiting, and rapid heart rate. Requires immediate medical attention.
spontaneous rib fracture:  Spontaneous rib fractures, often in older women, cause sudden chest pain, worsened by breathing or coughing. May lead to pneumothorax or hemothorax. Minimal trauma can trigger fractures.
gerd:  GERD is a digestive disorder causing stomach acid to irritate throat, with symptoms like heartburn, chest pain, regurgitation, and difficulty swallowing.
hiv (initial infection):  HIV: Initial infection may cause flu-like symptoms, such as fever, fatigue, rash, sore throat, swollen lymph nodes, within 2-4 weeks. Symptoms vary widely.
anemia:  Anemia is a condition with low red blood cells or hemoglobin, causing fatigue, weakness, pale skin, irregular heartbeat, and shortness of breath.
viral pharyngitis:  Viral pharyngitis is inflammation of the throat, caused by viruses, with symptoms of sore throat, cough, fever, and swollen lymph nodes.
inguinal hernia:  Inguinal hernia is the protrusion of abdominal tissue through a weakened groin area, causing discomfort, pain, or a visible bulge, which may become more pronounced when coughing, bending, or lifting heavy objects. Symptoms can vary in severity and frequency.
myasthenia gravis:  Myasthenia gravis is a neurological disorder causing muscle weakness, fatigue, and voluntary muscle dysfunction. Symptoms vary but often include eyelid drooping, double vision, and difficulty speaking, swallowing or breathing.
whooping cough:  Whooping cough is a highly contagious bacterial infection causing uncontrollable coughing fits, often with a "whoop" sound. Vaccination recommended.
anaphylaxis:  Anaphylaxis is a severe, life-threatening allergic reaction with symptoms like difficulty breathing, rapid heartbeat, and skin rashes. Swift medical attention required.
epiglottitis:  Epiglottitis is a severe inflammation of the epiglottis, causing difficulty breathing, swallowing, and speaking. Symptoms: sudden fever, sore throat, muffled voice, drooling.
guillain-barré syndrome:  Guillain-Barré syndrome is a rare disorder where the immune system attacks nerves, causing muscle weakness, numbness, and sometimes paralysis. Symptoms vary in severity and duration.
acute laryngitis:  Acute laryngitis is inflammation of the voice box, causing hoarseness, sore throat, cough, and sometimes fever. Lasts usually under 3 weeks.
croup:  Croup is a childhood respiratory infection causing barky cough, hoarseness, and harsh breathing sounds, often worsening at night.
psvt:  PSVT, or Primary Sclerosing Cholangitis, is a rare liver disease causing scarring and narrowing of bile ducts. Symptoms: fatigue, itching, jaundice, abdominal pain, and potential liver failure.
atrial fibrillation:  Atrial fibrillation is an irregular, often rapid heart rate that can cause poor blood flow, leading to symptoms like heart flutters, fatigue, and shortness of breath.
bronchiectasis:  Bronchiectasis is a lung disease involving abnormal widening of airways, causing chronic cough, phlegm production, and recurrent infections.
allergic sinusitis:  Allergic sinusitis is inflammation of sinuses due to allergens, causing symptoms like nasal congestion, runny nose, sneezing, and sinus pressure.
chagas:  Chagas Disease: Infection by "kissing bug," causes heart damage, symptoms include fever, swelling, fatigue, irregular heartbeat. Can be fatal if untreated.
scombroid food poisoning:  Scombroid poisoning is a foodborne illness from eating spoiled fish, causing symptoms like flushing, headache, rapid heartbeat, and diarrhea. Onset within minutes to hours.
myocarditis:  Myocarditis is inflammation of the heart muscle, causing chest pain, heart failure signs, and abnormal heart rhythms. Quick medical attention needed.
larygospasm:  Laryngospasm is a sudden, involuntary closure of the vocal cords, causing breathing difficulty, coughing, and high-pitched noises during inhalation.
acute dystonic reactions:  Acute dystonic reaction is a drug-induced movement disorder, characterized by sudden muscle spasms & involuntary movements, often affecting the face & neck.
localized edema:  Localized edema disease is a bacterial infection, mainly affecting aquatic animals. Symptoms: inflammation, fluid-filled lesions, skin sloughing.
sle:  Sjogren's (SLE) is an autoimmune disease causing dry eyes, mouth, joint pain, fatigue, and possible organ damage. Symptoms vary widely. Stay hydrated, manage symptoms with healthcare provider.
tuberculosis:  Tuberculosis is a bacterial infection, often affecting lungs; symptoms include cough, chest pain, fever, weight loss, and fatigue. Can be treated with antibiotics.
unstable angina:  Unstable angina is chest pain due to insufficient blood/oxygen to heart muscle, often sudden, increasing in severity or occurring at rest. It's a medical emergency.
stable angina:  Stable angina is chest pain or discomfort due to reduced blood flow to the heart muscle, typically during physical exertion or stress. Symptoms: tightness, heaviness, burning, or squeezing in the chest; possible radiation to left arm, neck, jaw, or back; shortness of breath; fatigue; nausea; anxiety.
ebola:  Ebola is a severe, often fatal virus causing fever, muscle pain, weakness, bleeding and organ failure. Symptoms appear 2-21 days after exposure.
acute otitis media:  Acute Otitis Media (AOM) is an ear infection with symptoms like ear pain, hearing loss, fever, and difficulty sleeping. It can also cause irritability and tugging at the ear.
panic attack:  A panic attack is a sudden, intense surge of fear or discomfort with symptoms like accelerated heart rate, trembling, sweating, and difficulty breathing. Lasts several minutes.
bronchospasm / acute asthma exacerbation:  Bronchospasm/asthma exacerbation: Narrowed airways, difficulty breathing, coughing, wheezing, chest tightness, rapid heartbeat.
bronchitis:  Bronchitis is an inflammation of the bronchi, causing cough, phlegm, and sometimes difficulty breathing. May also involve fever, fatigue, and chest discomfort.
acute copd exacerbation / infection:  Acute COPD exacerbation is a sudden worsening of chronic obstructive pulmonary disease, often due to infection, with symptoms like increased cough, shortness of breath, and mucus production.
pulmonary embolism:  Pulmonary embolism is a blockage, usually a blood clot, in lung arteries, causing difficulty breathing, chest pain, rapid heartbeat, and sometimes coughing up blood.
urti:  Upper Respiratory Tract Infection (URTI) is a viral or bacterial infection, causing symptoms like cough, sore throat, runny nose, and fatigue.
influenza:  Influenza, or flu, is a viral respiratory illness with symptoms like fever, cough, sore throat, runny/stuffy nose, muscle/body aches, headaches, fatigue.
pneumonia:  Pneumonia is an infection causing inflammation in the lungs, leading to cough, chest pain, fever, difficulty breathing, and fatigue.
acute rhinosinusitis:  Acute rhinosinusitis is inflammation of nasal sinuses, causing facial pain, thick mucus, congestion, cough, and fever for up to 4 weeks. Stay within word count.
chronic rhinosinusitis:  Chronic Rhinosinusitis is a long-term inflammation of sinuses, causing nasal congestion, discharge, facial pain, and loss of smell. May affect quality of life.
bronchiolitis:  Bronchiolitis is a respiratory infection, mainly in infants, causing inflammation & congestion in small airways, with symptoms like cough, wheezing, difficulty breathing.
pulmonary neoplasm:  Pulmonary neoplasm is a lung tumor, often cancerous, causing cough, chest pain, weight loss, and breathlessness. Early detection saves lives.
possible nstemi / stemi:  NSTEMI/STEMIs are heart conditions causing chest pain, shortness of breath, fatigue, nausea, rapid heartbeat, cold sweats, lightheadedness. Immediate medical attention required.
sarcoidosis:  Sarcoidosis is a rare inflammatory disease causing granulomas, leading to organ damage; symptoms vary by affected organs, often with no early signs.
pancreatic neoplasm:  Pancreatic neoplasm is a cancerous or noncancerous growth in the pancreas. Symptoms: jaundice, abdominal pain, weight loss, vomiting.
acute pulmonary edema:  Acute pulmonary edema is a serious condition causing fluid buildup in lungs, leading to shortness of breath, coughing, and possible pink frothy sputum. Rapid medical attention required.
pericarditis:  Pericarditis is inflammation of the lining around the heart, causing chest pain, often sharp and stabbing, sometimes with fever or difficulty breathing.
